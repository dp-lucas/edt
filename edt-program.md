# Minimalist EDT Training Program
### Escalating Density Training • Full-Body • 3 Days/Week

---

## Program Philosophy

This is a minimalist Escalating Density Training (EDT) plan adapted from Charles Staley's original framework. It is designed for someone working a 9-to-5 job who wants to stay strong, lean, and functional without bodybuilder-level time commitment.

Core principles:

- **Frequency.** Three workouts per week in ABA / BAB alternation. Across two weeks you complete six sessions: three A days and three B days.
- **Density over intensity.** Each main pair is performed for a fixed 15-minute Personal Record Zone (PR Zone). The goal is to accumulate more total reps over time, not to grind to failure.
- **The 5-rep cap.** Per Staley's original method, use a load you could perform for ~10 reps but stop every set at 5 reps maximum. This preserves form and lets you sustain work across the full 15 minutes.
- **Antagonist pairing.** Squat is paired with pull variations (Day A); Romanian Deadlift is paired with push variations (Day B). These are non-topographically related pairings — upper body with lower body — which Staley explicitly sanctions for full-body and fat loss programs to maximize total muscle recruitment per session. Pairing RDL with a pull movement would double-tax the posterior chain, so push is used instead. Practically, both pairings are designed around commercial gym setups: squat rack and pull-up bar share the same station; deadlift platform and dip bars are adjacent. No equipment transitions mid-block.
- **Movement coverage over the cycle.** Vertical pull, horizontal pull, vertical push, and horizontal push all rotate across A and B days, so over a month every plane is hit consistently.

## Load, Reps & Rest Rules

| Rule | Application |
|---|---|
| Load Selection | Choose a weight you could lift for ~10 reps to failure (10RM). You will never approach failure during a set. |
| Reps per Set | Start at 5 reps per set. Early in the block, 6–7 reps are fine if the weight feels light. Reduce to 4, 3, 2, 1 as fatigue accumulates. Stop a set early if form breaks before the rep target. |
| Rest Between Sets | Body-regulated, not clock-regulated. Use the three signals below to know the exact moment you're ready. Typically 15–25 sec early in the block, 30–45 sec mid-block, 45–90 sec late in the block — but follow the signals, not the clock. |
| PR Zone Duration | Set a timer for 15 minutes. Alternate between A1 and A2 until the timer ends. |
| Progression Trigger | Track total reps in each 15-minute block. When you exceed your previous record by 20%+, increase the load by ~5% the following session and reset the rep count. |
| Form Trumps Reps | If technique breaks, end the set immediately. Resume after a longer rest. Density is meaningless if form is sloppy. |

## Day A — Squat + Pull

Lower-body squat focus paired with vertical/horizontal pulling. Calves and core finish the session.

| Block | Pair | Exercise | Reps | Duration / Sets |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — Main Density Block | A1 | Back Squat | Cap at 5 | 15 min PR Zone |
| | A2 | Pull Variation — alternate each A day:<br>Day 1: Pull-Ups (vertical)<br>Day 2: Ring Rows (horizontal) | Cap at 5 | (paired with A1) |
| C — Calves | C1 | Standing Calf Raises | 8–12 | 2 sets |
| D — Core | D1 | Hanging Leg Raises | 8–12 | 2 sets |

**Exercise Notes — Day A**

- **Back Squat:** Move at whatever speed allows maximum safe rep accumulation. Control the descent enough to stay balanced and protect the knees — no crashing into the hole. Drive up with intent.
- **Pull-Ups:** Pull fast, lower under control. The 5-rep cap and no-failure rule handle form protection.
- **Ring Rows:** Pull fast to the chest, lower under control. Maintain a rigid plank throughout.
- **Standing Calf Raises:** Full range — complete stretch at the bottom, full contraction at the top.
- **Hanging Leg Raises:** Controlled movement. Momentum turns this into a hip-flexor exercise.

## Day B — Romanian Deadlift + Push

Hip-hinge focus paired with vertical/horizontal pushing. Calves and core finish the session.

| Block | Pair | Exercise | Reps | Duration / Sets |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A — Main Density Block | A1 | Romanian Deadlift | Cap at 5 | 15 min PR Zone |
| | A2 | Push Variation — alternate each B day:<br>Day 1: Dips (vertical)<br>Day 2: Push-Ups (horizontal) | Cap at 5 | (paired with A1) |
| C — Calves | C1 | Seated Calf Raises | 8–12 | 2 sets |
| D — Core | D1 | Ab Wheel Rollouts | 6–10 | 2 sets |

**Exercise Notes — Day B**

- **Romanian Deadlift:** Soft knees, bar drags the legs, hinge at the hips, neutral spine throughout. Stop the descent when you feel a strong hamstring stretch — usually mid-shin to just below the knee. Do NOT chase the floor. Move at a pace that allows clean reps throughout the block.
- **Dips:** Lower under control, drive up with intent. Stop at upper-arms parallel if shoulders are sensitive.
- **Push-Ups:** Maintain a rigid plank throughout. Lower under control, press up fast.
- **Seated Calf Raises:** Full range — stretch at the bottom is where the soleus responds most.
- **Ab Wheel Rollouts:** Controlled extension, core stays firing throughout. Shorten the range if you can't maintain the position.

## Full Month Schedule

ABA / BAB rotation across 4 weeks. The pull and push variations alternate so every movement plane is trained twice per fortnight. Recommended training days: Monday / Wednesday / Friday — but any non-consecutive 3-day pattern works.

| Week | Mon | Wed | Fri |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Day A — Squat + Pull-Ups | Day B — RDL + Dips | Day A — Squat + Ring Rows |
| Week 2 | Day B — RDL + Push-Ups | Day A — Squat + Pull-Ups | Day B — RDL + Dips |
| Week 3 | Day A — Squat + Ring Rows | Day B — RDL + Push-Ups | Day A — Squat + Pull-Ups |

## Progression & Tracking

**How to track:**
After every session, write down two numbers for the 15-minute PR Zone: total reps of A1 and total reps of A2. Example for Day A: "Squat 60 / Pull-Up 32". This is the only data point that matters for progression.

**When to add load:**
When you exceed your previous best total reps in a PR Zone by 20% or more, increase the load on that lift by approximately 5% the next session. If you exceed your baseline by 40% or more — most likely after a deload week or during the first few weeks of neural adaptation — increase by 10% instead. Reset your rep count and start building density again. This is the entire progression model — no percentages, no periodization charts.

**What to expect:**
Density gains come quickly in the first 2–3 weeks (rapid neural adaptation), then slow. By weeks 4–8 you'll typically be adding load every 2–3 sessions. After that, gains become steadier and slower — which is normal and exactly what "staying fit" looks like.

**Deload guidance:**
Every 6–8 weeks, take one week where you reduce the PR Zone to 10 minutes instead of 15. No load increase, no rep chasing. Just movement quality. This prevents accumulated fatigue from compromising the next training block.

## Safety & Execution Notes

- **Warm-up.** 5 minutes minimum before each session. For Day A: hip openers, bodyweight squats, scapular pull-ups. For Day B: hip hinge drills with a dowel, bodyweight push-ups, band pull-aparts.
- **Knowing when to transition between exercises.** For your upper/lower pairings, the limiting factor between sets is cardiovascular, not local muscle fatigue — your legs rest completely during pulling, and your upper body rests completely during squatting. Use three signals: (1) **Breathing test** — when you can take one full controlled breath through the nose and brace with it, you're ready. Still mouth-gasping, wait. (2) **Bracing test** — before stepping back under the squat bar or pulling the RDL, take your breath and brace. If the brace feels solid, go. If it feels weak or rushed, wait 10 more seconds. (3) **Quality check** — ask "can I do 5 clean reps right now?" Honest yes, go. Honest no, wait 10–15 seconds and ask again. These signals naturally produce transitions of ~15–25 sec early in the block, ~30–45 sec mid-block, and ~45–90 sec late in the block. The 15-minute clock provides all the pressure you need not to over-rest; the signals tell you the minimum safe point to move.
- **Romanian Deadlift form.** Soft knees, bar drags the legs, hinge at the hips, neutral spine throughout. Stop the descent when you feel a strong hamstring stretch — usually mid-shin to just below the knee. Do NOT chase the floor.
- **The 5-rep cap is a strong default, not an absolute ceiling.** Early in the block, when fatigue is low, sets of 6–7 are fine if the weight feels unusually light. Taper down to 5, then 4, 3, 2, 1 as the session progresses. The rule exists to prevent form breakdown from grinding — not to cap reps when you genuinely have them.
- **Pull-up regression.** If you can't yet do 5 strict pull-ups, use a band assist or do negatives (jump up, lower under control). Same logic for dips — bench dips or band-assisted are acceptable substitutes.
- **Recovery comes first.** Three sessions per week is the prescription. Adding a fourth session because you feel fresh is how minimalist plans become non-minimalist plans. If life is busy, drop to 2x/week — never more than 3x.
- **Sleep, food, walking.** None of this matters if you sleep 5 hours and eat poorly. The minimalist program assumes a baseline of 7+ hours sleep, adequate protein (~0.7–1 g/lb bodyweight), and daily walking. Without those, the program will still work — just slower.
